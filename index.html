Portfolio Monte Carlo

An interactive simulation of how a venture-style portfolio could perform. Each investment buys a fixed 2% stake at entry, dilutes through follow-on rounds, exits at a category-dependent valuation, and is discounted back to present value. The full portfolio is re-run across 1,000 independent simulations on every change. All parameters and assumptions below are editable — change them to test the model against your own view.

Methodology

  • Entry: every company is acquired at 2% ownership for the configured entry cost. Implied post-money entry valuation = cost ÷ 2%.
  • Outcome category: each company is independently assigned an outcome (failure, small, medium, large, unicorn) by sampling from the probability table.
  • Exit valuation: sampled from a log-normal centered on the category median with σ = 0.5. Mean of the draw ≈ median × 1.13, with a long right tail.
  • Dilution: fraction of original 2% lost across follow-on rounds. Bigger winners raise more capital and dilute more (unicorn ≈ 70%, small ≈ 25% by default). Gaussian noise σ = 5% per draw.
  • Proceeds: exit valuation × (2% × (1 − dilution)). Zero for failures.
  • NPV: proceeds × 1 / (1 + r)^t. Margin per company = NPV − entry cost.
  • Monte Carlo: 1,000 independent portfolio simulations re-run on every parameter change. Per-company averages use the full 1,000 × N pool.
  • Not modeled: follow-on capital, pro-rata, fund expenses/carry, exit timing variance (all exits assumed at year t), management fees, recycling, write-down timing.
